IELTS Speaking

Lesson 13

Part 3 Intensive &
Difficult Question Control

Main Goals

Part 3 is not about telling a personal story.

Part 3 is about: Opinion → Reason → Example → Finish.

1
Improve your weakest part: Part 3.
2
Answer abstract questions more clearly.
3
Stay calm with difficult questions.
4
Strengthen Band 5.5–6 style speaking structure.

Today's Core Rule

Do not give many weak ideas.

Give ONE clear idea and develop it.

The 3 Answer Shapes for Part 3

Train to answer difficult questions in a safe and organised way.

1. Opinion Shape

  • I think...
  • One reason is that...
  • For example...
  • So overall...

2. Change Shape

  • In the past...
  • Nowadays...
  • One reason is that...
  • So I think...

3. Balanced Shape

  • I think it can be both...
  • On one hand...
  • On the other hand...
  • So overall...

Structure 1

Opinion + Reason + Example + Result

Use this for:

"Why" questions • "Should" questions • "What skills" questions

Model Answer

Q: Why do some people learn skills faster than others?

I think some people learn skills faster because they practise more regularly. One reason is that repeated practice helps people improve step by step. For example, a student who studies every day usually improves faster than someone who studies only sometimes. So overall, regular practice makes a big difference.

Structure 2

Past + Present + Difference + Reason

Use this for:

"Has... changed?" • "Do people... differently now?" • "Are young people different now?"

Model Answer

Q: Has communication changed?

Yes, I think communication has changed a lot. In the past, people usually communicated more face to face. Nowadays, many people depend on phones and social media. One reason is that technology has become part of daily life. So overall, communication is now faster but sometimes less personal.

Structure 3

Positive Side + Negative Side + Position

Use this for:

"Is social media helpful or harmful?" • "Is technology always useful?"

Model Answer

Q: Is social media helpful or harmful?

I think social media can be both helpful and harmful. On one hand, it helps people communicate and find useful information quickly. On the other hand, it can waste time and affect people negatively if they use it too much. For example, many young people learn useful things online, but they can also copy unhealthy habits. So overall, I think it is useful if people use it carefully.

Topic Area 1

Skills & Learning

Q: Why do some people learn skills faster than others?

I think some people learn skills faster because they practise more regularly. One reason is that repeated practice helps people build confidence and improve step by step. For example, someone who uses English every day usually becomes more fluent than a person who studies only once in a while. So overall, regular practice is one of the biggest reasons.

Q: Is practice more important than talent?

Yes, I think practice is more important than talent in many situations. One reason is that talent alone is not enough without effort and consistency. For example, a talented person may still stay at a low level if they do not practise seriously. So I believe practice is usually the key to long-term improvement.

Q: What skills are important for young people today?

I think communication skills, computer skills, and problem-solving are very important for young people today. One reason is that these skills are useful in both education and future work. For example, many jobs now require people to use technology and express their ideas clearly. So overall, practical and communication-based skills matter a lot in modern life.

Q: Should schools teach more practical skills?

Yes, I think schools should teach more practical skills. One reason is that students need abilities for real life, not only academic knowledge. For example, time management, communication, and computer use are very useful in university and future jobs. So I believe practical skills should have a bigger place in education.

Topic Area 2

Technology & Social Media

Q: Has social media changed communication?

Yes, definitely. I think social media has changed communication a lot. In the past, people usually communicated more directly and less frequently. Nowadays, people can communicate instantly through apps and platforms. One reason is that technology has made communication much faster and easier. So overall, communication is now quicker, although sometimes less personal.

Q: Is technology always helpful for learning?

I think technology is helpful for learning in many ways, but not always. One reason is that it gives students easy access to information, videos, and practice materials. For example, someone preparing for IELTS can study independently and find support online. However, technology can also become a distraction if it is not used carefully. So overall, it is helpful when people use it with discipline.

Q: Are young people too dependent on technology?

Yes, I think many young people are quite dependent on technology now. One reason is that phones and online tools are part of study, communication, and entertainment. For example, many students use technology for schoolwork, but they also spend a lot of time on social media and videos. So overall, technology is useful, but dependence has clearly increased.

Q: Is social media more helpful or more harmful?

I think social media can be both helpful and harmful. On one hand, it allows people to communicate quickly and find useful ideas. On the other hand, it can waste time and affect people negatively if they compare themselves too much with others online. For example, many users learn useful things from social media, but others develop unhealthy habits because of it. So overall, I think it depends on how people use it.

Topic Area 3

Future & Planning

Q: Why do some people make long-term plans?

I think some people make long-term plans because they want a clearer direction in life. One reason is that planning helps people stay organised and more motivated. For example, a student who wants to study abroad may create a plan for exams, language improvement, and university applications. So overall, long-term planning helps people move toward important goals.

Q: Why do some people avoid long-term planning?

I think some people avoid long-term planning because the future feels uncertain and difficult to control. One reason is that they may be afraid that their plans will change or fail. For example, if someone is not sure about work or study, they may prefer short-term decisions instead. So overall, uncertainty is one of the main reasons.

Q: Do young people feel more pressure about the future now?

Yes, I think many young people feel more pressure about the future now. One reason is that modern life requires them to improve continuously and make important decisions quite early. For example, many students feel pressure about language skills, university, work, and financial success. So overall, future pressure seems stronger than before.

Q: What skills will be important in the future?

I think communication skills, adaptability, and digital skills will be especially important in the future. One reason is that work and education are becoming more connected to technology and fast change. For example, many jobs now require people to learn new tools and communicate clearly with others. So overall, flexible and practical skills will become even more valuable.

Topic Area 4

Influence & Communication

Q: Why do some people influence others more strongly than others?

I think some people influence others more strongly because they are more confident, visible, or respected. One reason is that people usually listen more carefully to someone they trust or admire. For example, teachers, parents, or public figures can strongly affect how young people think. So overall, trust and confidence are two major parts of influence.

Q: Do family members influence people more than friends?

I think family members often influence people more deeply, especially in early life. One reason is that family usually shapes values, habits, and goals from childhood. However, friends can also have strong influence during teenage years and adulthood. For example, a close friend may affect someone’s behaviour or decisions in daily life. So overall, family tends to have a deeper effect, but both are important.

Q: Are role models important?

Yes, I think role models are very important. One reason is that they can give people motivation and a clearer idea of what they want to become. For example, a student may work harder after seeing someone successful in the same field. So overall, role models can positively influence people’s goals and behaviour.

Q: Is influence always positive?

No, I do not think influence is always positive. One reason is that people can also be affected by bad examples, especially online. For example, some young people may copy unhealthy behaviours from influencers or trends on social media. So overall, influence can be positive or negative depending on the source.

Topic Area 5

Modern Life & Change

Q: Do people communicate differently now than in the past?

Yes, definitely. In the past, communication was usually slower and more direct. Nowadays, people often communicate through messages, apps, and social media. One reason is that technology has become part of daily routine. So overall, communication is now much faster, but sometimes less personal than before.

Q: Is modern life better than life in the past?

I think modern life is better in some ways, but it also has disadvantages. On one hand, people now have better access to information, technology, and communication. On the other hand, life has become busier and more stressful. For example, many people save time through technology, but they also feel more pressure in daily life. So overall, modern life is more convenient, but not always calmer.

Q: Do people have less patience now?

Yes, I think many people have less patience now than before. One reason is that modern technology makes everything faster, so people become used to getting results quickly. For example, many users expect instant replies, fast videos, and quick solutions. So overall, modern habits may reduce patience in some areas of life.

Q: Are practical skills more useful now than before?

Yes, I think practical skills are very useful now, perhaps even more than before in some situations. One reason is that modern life requires people to solve problems quickly and adapt to changing conditions. For example, communication, digital ability, and time management are useful in both study and work. So overall, practical skills have become extremely valuable.

Part 3 Checklist

A STRONG Answer:

  • includes a clear opinion
  • gives ONE main reason
  • gives ONE example
  • has a clear ending
  • no jumping between many ideas
  • not very short

A WEAK Answer:

  • no opinion given
  • too many mixed ideas
  • no example
  • no structure
  • unclear grammar hides meaning

Reusable Sentences:

  • "For example, students usually improve faster when they practise every day."
  • "I think technology is helpful, but only if people use it in a balanced way."
  • "In modern life, communication and digital skills have become very important."
  • "I believe schools should prepare students for real life, not only exams."

Review + Homework

Final Review:

Today’s lesson is about control in difficult questions.

You do not need:

  • difficult vocabulary
  • very complex grammar
  • many weak ideas

You need:

  • one clear opinion
  • one clear reason
  • one clear example
  • one clear ending

Voice Homework

Prepare voice answers for these 4 Part 3 questions:

  • 1. Why do some people learn skills faster than others?
  • 2. Is social media more helpful or more harmful?
  • 3. Do young people feel more pressure about the future now?
  • 4. Should schools teach more practical skills?

Homework Rules:

  • answer in 4–6 sentences
  • start with your opinion
  • give one reason & one example
  • finish clearly
  • do not jump between many ideas

Master Part 3.

Part 3 is where you show control, not just vocabulary.

A simple, clear, well-structured answer is always better than a complex, confusing one.

Opinion + Reason + Example + Finish

That is enough to make Part 3 much stronger.
