IELTS Speaking

Lesson 10

Part 1 Natural Fluency &
Quick Answer Control

Main Goals

Part 1 is short, but it is important. You do not need a big story.

1
Answer naturally and clearly.
2
Stop very short AND overlong confused answers.
3
Give 3–4 good sentences quickly.
4
Improve immediate speaking without panic.

Today's Core Rule

Part 1 answer = short, clear, natural

Not too short: Yes. No. Maybe.
Not too long: many ideas, topic changes, confusion.

Warm-Up

Answer these 4 quick personal questions:

1

Do you usually wake up early?

2

Do you like quiet places?

3

Do you often use your phone?

4

Do you enjoy studying alone?

Teacher Note

Do not stop too much. Do not search for difficult words.

Answer simply and clearly.

The Part 1 Golden Formula

1. Direct Answer
2. Reason
3. Detail / Example
4. Short Finish

Model Structure A

  • "Yes, I do."
  • "One reason is that..."
  • "For example..."
  • "So overall..."

Model Structure B

  • "I usually..."
  • "This is because..."
  • "For instance..."
  • "So that’s why..."

Keep It Direct & Use ONE Main Idea

1. Personal & Direct

Part 1 asks about YOUR life, habits, and opinions. Sound personal.

Q: Do you like music?

Bad (Too general/Confused)

"Yes, music is important because people in the world like many songs and social media and different feelings and culture and everything."

Better (Personal/Direct)

"Yes, I do. I listen to music quite often because it helps me relax. For example, I usually listen to it when I want to feel calmer or take a break. So music is an important part of my daily life."

2. ONE Main Idea

Do not answer with 5 hobbies. Choose one or two only.

Q: What do you do in your free time?

Bad (Too many ideas)

"I play guitar and swimming and walking and fashion and social media and many things and TV and friends."

Better (One developed idea)

"In my free time, I mostly enjoy creating fashion content for social media. I like it because it feels creative and personal at the same time. For example, I sometimes plan outfits and shoot short videos. So that is one of my favourite free-time activities."

Part 1 Answers Should Be 3–4 Sentences

1 sentence = too short 3–4 sentences = very good Too many = confusing

Model Target

Q: Do hobbies help people relax?

Sentence 1 (Direct): Yes, definitely.
Sentence 2 (Reason): I think hobbies help people relax because they give the mind a break from daily stress.
Sentence 3 (Detail): For example, activities like walking or listening to music can make people feel calmer.
Sentence 4 (Ending): So overall, hobbies are very useful for mental comfort.

Use Simple Fillers Naturally

Use them once to structure your answer, then continue.

1. Starters

  • • Yes, I do.
  • • No, not really.
  • • Most of the time, yes.
  • • It depends, but generally yes.
  • • Usually, yes.

2. Thinking Phrases

  • • Let me think.
  • • I’d say...
  • • To be honest...
  • • In my case...
  • • Well, actually...

3. Reason Phrases

  • • One reason is that...
  • • This is because...
  • • Mainly because...
  • • I think the main reason is...

4. Detail Phrases

  • • For example...
  • • For instance...
  • • In my daily life...
  • • Especially when...

5. Ending Phrases

  • • So that’s why...
  • • So overall...
  • • That’s the main reason for me.
  • • So I’d say yes.

Do not overuse:

"actually actually actually" / "I think I think I think" / "because because because"

Smart Answering Tactics

Handling Questions You Do Not Love

Even if you don't know exactly what to say: 1) Answer simply, 2) Give a general reason, 3) Give a small real-life example.

Q: Do you enjoy reading?

"Yes, in some ways, I do. I especially enjoy reading when the topic is useful or interesting for me. For example, I prefer practical or informative content rather than long stories. So I’d say I like reading when it has a clear purpose."

Do Not Memorise One Fixed Answer

Examiners ask connected questions. Answers should sound related, not repeated word for word.

Q: What kind of work do you do?

A: I work in civil engineering and architecture. It is a field I find both practical and creative.

Q: Why did you choose this job?

A: I chose it because I have been interested in architecture and design for a long time. I also like work that feels meaningful.

Q: What do you like most about your job?

A: What I like most is the creative side of it. I enjoy thinking about design and useful improvements.

Same topic, different answer. No repetition.

Controlled Practice

Upgrade these short answers using the Golden Formula.

Short Answer

"Yes, I do."

Improved Answer

Yes, I do. I enjoy it because... For example... So...

Short Answer

"No, not really."

Improved Answer

No, not really. I don’t do it very often because... For example... So...

Short Answer

"Sometimes."

Improved Answer

Sometimes, yes. It depends on... For example... So...

Teacher asks: "Do you like quiet places?" → Student answers using formula.

High-Value Model Answers

Topic: Work

Q: What kind of work do you do?

Safe Answer

I work in civil engineering and architecture. I like this field because it is both practical and creative. For example, I enjoy design and planning. So it is an important field for me.

Better Answer

I work in civil engineering and architecture. I like this field because it combines practical work with creativity, which makes it more interesting for me. For example, I enjoy design, planning, and thinking about how spaces can be improved. So overall, it is a field that suits my interests very well.

Q: Why did you choose this job?

Safe Answer

I chose this job because I have liked architecture for a long time. It matches my interests and my future goals. So that is the main reason.

Better Answer

I chose this job because I have been interested in architecture and design for a long time. I like the idea of creating something useful, not only something that looks good. It also matches my future goals, which is why I feel strongly connected to this field. So I think it was the right choice for me.

Topic: Study

Q: Do you prefer studying alone or with others?

Safe Answer

I usually prefer studying alone. It helps me focus better and work at my own speed. For example, I can repeat things when I need to. So studying alone is usually better for me.

Better Answer

I usually prefer studying alone because it helps me focus better and work at my own speed. When I study alone, I can repeat difficult points without pressure and organise my time more freely. For example, if I want to practise speaking or review vocabulary, I can do it in my own way. So overall, independent study works better for me most of the time.

Q: What helps you study well?

Safe Answer

Clear explanation helps me study well. Practice is also important for me. For example, when I repeat something aloud, I remember it better. So that really helps me.

Better Answer

Clear explanation helps me study well, and regular practice is also very important for me. I understand things more easily when the explanation is simple and practical. For example, if I repeat something aloud or use it in speaking, I usually remember it better. So I learn best when the study feels active, not passive.

Topic: Free Time

Q: What do you do in your free time?

Better Answer

In my free time, I mostly enjoy creating fashion content for social media. I like it because it feels creative and personal at the same time. For example, I sometimes plan outfits, think about style ideas, and shoot short videos or reels. So I would say it is one of the activities I enjoy most.

Q: Do you prefer to spend free time alone or with others?

Better Answer

It depends on my mood, but I often enjoy spending free time alone because it helps me relax and focus on my own interests. For example, if I want to think, create content, or just rest mentally, being alone feels more comfortable. However, I also enjoy being with close friends or family sometimes. So I would say I like a balance, but I often prefer some quiet time.

Topic: Hometown & Technology

Q: What kind of place is your hometown?

Better Answer

My hometown is not extremely big, but it feels familiar and comfortable to me. Daily life there is usually calmer and simpler than in a huge city, which I appreciate. I think that kind of atmosphere makes people feel more connected to the place. So overall, I would describe it as familiar, practical, and emotionally important.

Q: Do you use technology a lot every day?

Better Answer

Yes, I use technology a lot every day. It is part of my communication, study, and even some creative work. For example, I use it to organise tasks, search for information, and create or watch content. So overall, it is a very normal and necessary part of my daily life.

  • 4. Do you prefer tea or coffee?

  • 6. Is your neighbourhood quiet?
  • 7. Do you like taking photos?
  • 8. Are hobbies important?

Part 1 Rescue Technique

If you get stuck, say a rescue phrase and continue:

• Let me think.

• I’d say...

• In my case...

• To be honest...

• Most of the time, yes.

• It depends, but generally...

Correction Focus Today:

too short too many ideas no reason no example unclear answer repetition

Micro-Paraphrasing Practice

Teach your brain to quickly change simple words to Band 6+ words.

like enjoy / prefer
important useful / valuable
good helpful / interesting
big large
relax feel calm
help support / improve

Example: Are hobbies important?

"Yes, hobbies are important."

"Yes, I think hobbies are very valuable."

Review + Homework

Today's Most Important Rules:

Short does not mean weak.

Long does not mean good.

Clear is best.

In Part 1:

  • Answer clearly
  • Give one reason
  • Add one detail
  • Stop before you become confused

Voice Homework

Try to answer each in 3–4 sentences:

  • 1. What kind of work do you do?
  • 2. Why did you choose this job?
  • 3. What do you do in your free time?
  • 4. Do hobbies help people relax?
  • 5. Do you use social media regularly?
  • 6. What do you usually use it for?
  • 7. Do you prefer studying alone or with others?
  • 8. What helps you study well?

Homework Rules:

  • Answer directly
  • Give one reason & one example/detail
  • Do not jump to many ideas
  • Do not make the answer too short

Keep It Quick & Clear.

In Part 1, you do not need perfect or difficult English.

You need a quick, clear, natural answer.

  • Answer directly.
  • Give one reason.
  • Add one detail.
  • Finish clearly.

That is enough to make your speaking stronger!
