Main Goals

You do not need 100 different lives for IELTS.

You need a small number of strong personal ideas that can be used in many topics.

1
Prepare for the most common IELTS topics.
2
Build a strong personal answer bank.
3
Reuse your real life across many questions.
4
Make speaking easier, faster, and more natural.

Today's Core Rule

Do not invent too much. Use your real life and recycle it smartly.

Recycling Your Life (Bank 1 & 2)

Bank 1: Work / Field

Main Idea to Memorize:

"I work in civil engineering and architecture, and I’m especially interested in design, restoration, and useful creative work."

Can be used to answer:

  • What kind of work do you do?
  • Why did you choose this field?
  • What do you like about your work?
  • Describe a job you find interesting
  • Describe a skill related to your work
  • What skills are important in your field?
Bank 2: Future Goal

Main Idea to Memorize:

"I want to improve my English and get a suitable IELTS band because I want to continue my studies abroad, especially in Italy."

Can be used to answer:

  • Why are you learning English?
  • Describe a goal you want to achieve
  • Describe something you are preparing for
  • Why do people study abroad?
  • What skills are important for the future?
  • Describe a future plan

Part 2: Influence Story

Describe a person who influenced you.

Model Answer (Band 7+):

I’d like to talk about my sister, who influenced me in a very positive way. I have known her all my life because she is a close family member, and I have always respected her.

The reason she influenced me so much is that she studied architecture, which is a field I became interested in later. When I was younger, I had the chance to visit her university in another city.

While I was there, I saw her projects, some of her classmates, and the general atmosphere of the university. I remember feeling really impressed because everything looked creative, serious, and meaningful at the same time.

Before that visit, architecture was just an idea for me, but after that, it started to feel like a real future path. That is why this experience stayed in my mind and why my sister had such a strong influence on me.

Part 2: Goals & Skills

Describe a goal you want to achieve.

A goal I would really like to achieve is getting a suitable IELTS band score and improving my English speaking enough for my future studies.

This goal is very important to me because I want to continue my education abroad, especially in Italy. I have been thinking about this plan for a while, so it is not just a short-term idea.

At the moment, I am working on my speaking, trying to organise my answers better, and building more confidence step by step.

I know this goal will require patience and regular practice, but I also believe it can open important academic and personal opportunities for me. That is why this goal matters so much to me.

Describe a skill you would like to improve.

The skill I would most like to improve is English speaking. This is very important for me because I need it for IELTS and for my future academic plans.

In the past, I often had ideas in my mind, but I found it difficult to express them clearly and in an organised way.

Now, I am practising more regularly and learning how to build stronger answers with better structure.

I think this skill is valuable because it can help me in university, communication, and future opportunities. So overall, improving my speaking is one of the most important things I am focusing on now.

Part 2: Free Time Activity

Describe a free-time activity you enjoy.

Model Answer (Content Creation):

A free-time activity I really enjoy is creating fashion-related content for social media. I enjoy it because it gives me a creative way to express my taste and ideas.

Usually, this includes thinking about visual style, planning outfits, and sometimes creating short content.

What I like most is that it combines creativity with personal interest, so it never feels boring to me. It also gives me a sense of motivation because I feel that I am producing something, not only consuming content.

That is why this activity is enjoyable for me and why I like spending time on it.

Part 3: Education & Goals

Area 1: Skills

"Why do some people learn skills faster than others?"

"I think some people learn skills faster because they practise more regularly. One reason is that repeated practice helps people improve step by step and become more confident. For example, a student who uses English every day usually improves faster than someone who studies only once in a while. So overall, regular practice makes a big difference."

Area 1: Schools

"Should schools teach more practical skills?"

"Yes, I think schools should teach more practical skills. One reason is that students need useful abilities for daily life and future work, not only academic information. For example, communication, time management, and problem-solving are important in many situations outside the classroom. So I believe practical skills should have a bigger place in education."

Area 2: Future Goals

"Why do many students want to study abroad?"

"I think many students want to study abroad because it gives them better educational and personal opportunities. One reason is that living in another country can help people become more independent and gain wider experience. For example, students may improve their language skills, education, and confidence at the same time. So overall, studying abroad can be valuable in many ways."

Part 3: Technology & Influence

Area 3: Social Media

"Has social media changed communication?"

"Yes, I think social media has changed communication a lot. In the past, communication was usually slower and more direct, but now people can connect instantly. One reason this matters is that communication has become much faster and more constant than before. So overall, social media has made communication easier, although sometimes less personal."

Area 4: People

"Why do some people influence others more strongly?"

"I think some people influence others more strongly because they are more confident or more respected. One reason is that people usually listen more carefully to someone they admire or trust. For example, a family member or teacher can strongly affect a young person’s goals and way of thinking. So overall, influence is often connected to trust and respect."

Area 4: Negative Influence

"Is influence always positive?"

"No, I do not think influence is always positive. One reason is that people can also be affected by bad examples, especially through social media. For example, some young people may copy behaviours that are not useful or responsible. So I think influence can be positive or negative depending on the source."

High-Value Reusable Phrases

P1
  • "I usually..."
  • "One reason is that..."
  • "For example..."
  • "So overall..."
P2
  • "I’d like to talk about..."
  • "What made it important was..."
  • "I remember feeling..."
  • "That is why it stayed in my mind."
P3
  • "I think..."
  • "One reason is that..."
  • "For example..."
  • "So I would say..."

Review + Homework

Final Review:

Today’s lesson is about building security.

The more familiar your content is, the easier it becomes to speak clearly.

You do not need new complicated ideas. You need:

  • familiar topics
  • clear structure
  • real examples
  • confident reuse

Voice Homework

Prepare voice answers for these 4 topics:

  • 1. What kind of work do you do?
  • 2. Describe a person who influenced you.
  • 3. Describe a goal you want to achieve.
  • 4. Why do many students want to study abroad?

Homework Rules:

  • use your real life
  • keep one clear idea
  • do not jump between many topics
  • give one reason & one example
  • finish clearly

Build Security.

The secret to strong IELTS speaking isn't inventing perfect stories for every question.

It's taking your real life and reusing it smartly.

Master your core ideas, and you will never panic in the exam.
