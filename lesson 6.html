Lesson 6

Giving Better Explanations

Lesson Aims

Today we will focus on how to:

1
Explain ideas more clearly
2
Talk about causes and effects
3
Talk about problems and solutions
4
Give more developed Part 3 answers

Quick Review

Part 2 Structure:

  • introduce the topic
  • give details
  • explain why it is important
  • explain the result or effect

Part 3 Structure:

  • opinion
  • reason
  • example
  • final sentence

Today’s Focus

We are learning how to explain ideas more deeply and give more developed answers.

Explaining Causes and Effects

Useful language:

This happens because… One reason is that… As a result,… That can lead to… This affects…

Model Answer

Q: Why do some students feel nervous before exams?

I think many students feel nervous before exams because they are afraid of making mistakes or getting a low score. As a result, they may lose confidence and feel more pressure. That can affect their performance during the exam.

Practice: Causes and Effects

Answer these questions:

1

Why do some students feel nervous before speaking exams?

2

Try to use:

because… one reason is that… as a result… that can lead to…

Talking About Problems & Solutions

Useful language:

One problem is that… Another problem is that… A good solution is… One way to solve this is… This can help because…

Model Answer

Q: What problems do students face when learning English?

One problem is that many students are afraid of speaking. Another problem is that they forget vocabulary quickly. A good solution is daily speaking practice and regular review. This can help because students become more confident and remember words better.

Practice: Problems & Solutions

Answer these questions:

1

What problems do students face in IELTS speaking?

2

How can students improve their vocabulary?

Try to use:

One problem is that… A good solution is… This can help because…

Giving Longer Part 3 Answers

Use this structure:

Main idea Cause/Problem Example Solution/Result

Model Question

Why do some people not improve in speaking, even after studying for a long time?

I think some people do not improve in speaking because they focus too much on grammar and not enough on real speaking practice. For example, they may study many rules but never record themselves or speak aloud. A good solution is to practice speaking every day, even for a short time. As a result, they can become more natural and confident.

Practice: Longer Part 3 Answers

1

Why do some students improve faster than others?

2

Why is speaking often harder than reading or listening?

3

How can teachers help students speak more confidently?

Remember to include:

main idea cause or problem example solution or result

Part 2: New Topic

Describe a change that improved your life

You should say:

  • what the change was
  • when it happened
  • why it happened
  • and explain how it improved your life

Part 2 structure:

  1. What the change was
  2. When it happened
  3. Why it happened
  4. How it helped me
  5. How I feel about it now

Useful phrases:

One important change in my life was… It happened when… The reason was that… This change helped me because… Since then,… I feel that it was a very positive change.

Part 2 Model Answer

Topic: Describe a change that improved your life

One important change in my life was starting to improve my English more seriously. It happened when I decided to prepare for the IELTS exam and focus on my future goals. The reason was that I want to study abroad and build a better future for myself. This change helped me because I became more focused, more motivated, and more confident. Since then, I have understood how important regular practice is.

Part 3: More Developed Discussion

Useful language for deeper answers:

This happens because… Another point is that… For instance,… As a result,… A possible solution is… This is important because…

Model Question

Why is it difficult for many people to make changes in their lives?

Model Answer

I think it is difficult for many people to make changes in their lives because change often feels uncomfortable or risky. Another point is that some people are afraid of failure. For instance, a student may want to improve their English but feel nervous about speaking. A possible solution is to start with small daily practice. As a result, the change becomes easier and more realistic.

Part 3 Practice

Answer these questions:

1

Why is it difficult for some people to change their habits?

2

What changes can help students become more successful?

3

Do you think technology has improved people’s lives? Why?

Try to use:

cause example result solution

Useful Vocabulary (1/2)

change = değişim

Ex: A positive change can improve a person’s life.

cause = sebep olmak / neden

Ex: Stress can cause students to forget what they know.

effect = etki, sonuç

Ex: Social media has a strong effect on young people.

solution = çözüm

Ex: A simple solution is to review vocabulary every day.

habit = alışkanlık

Ex: Daily speaking practice is a useful habit.

motivation = motivasyon

Ex: Strong motivation helps students improve faster.

pressure = baskı

Ex: Too much pressure can affect speaking performance.

confidence = özgüven

Ex: Practice helps students build confidence.

progress = ilerleme

Ex: Small daily study can lead to real progress.

routine = rutin

Ex: A healthy study routine supports regular learning.

More Useful Vocabulary (2/2)

improve = geliştirmek

Ex: Students need to improve step by step.

performance = performans

Ex: Confidence can improve speaking performance.

stress = stres

Ex: Stress can make it hard to think clearly.

effective = etkili

Ex: Recording answers is an effective method.

practical = pratik

Ex: Speaking is a practical skill.

support = destek

Ex: Teacher support can help students feel calmer.

goal = hedef

Ex: A clear goal gives students direction.

manage = yönetmek

Ex: It is important to manage time and stress.

result = sonuç

Ex: Hard work usually leads to a good result.

specific = belirli, özel

Ex: A specific example makes the answer stronger.

Useful Speaking Phrases

I think this happens because… One problem is that… Another point is that… For instance,… As a result,… A good solution is… One important change in my life was… Since then,… This helped me because… I feel that this was a positive change.
