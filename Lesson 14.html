IELTS Speaking

Lesson 14

Final Exam Readiness,
Confidence & Last Control

Main Goals

Today is not for learning many new things.

Today is for staying calm, speaking clearly, using safe structures, and showing the best possible version of your English.

1
Prepare for the real IELTS Speaking exam.
2
Gain final confidence and control.
3
Polish your strongest speaking strategies.
4
Reduce panic before the exam.

Today's Core Rule

Do not try to sound perfect.

Try to sound clear, calm, and organised.

Final Speaking Priorities

1. Speak clearly

2. Do not rush

3. Answer the question directly

4. Focus on one main idea

5. Give a reason or example

6. Keep going even if there is a mistake

Final Reminder

Part 1 Strategy

In Part 1:

  • answer directly
  • give one reason
  • add one small detail
  • stop before the answer becomes confused

Best Structure:

  • • Yes / No / Usually / Sometimes
  • • One reason is that...
  • • For example...
  • • So...

Model

"Yes, I do. One reason is that it helps me relax after a busy day. For example, I usually listen to music when I want to feel calmer. So it is part of my daily routine."

Final Reminder

Part 2 Strategy

In Part 2:

  • choose one clear topic direction
  • do not jump between many ideas
  • speak in order
  • add feeling & result
  • finish clearly

Best Structure:

  • • I’d like to talk about...
  • • This happened when...
  • • What happened was...
  • • I felt...
  • • That is why it is important to me.

Model

"I’d like to talk about improving my English speaking skill. This has become very important for me because I need IELTS for my future studies. At the moment, I am practising regularly and trying to organise my answers better. I feel more motivated now because this goal is connected to my future. That is why this skill matters so much to me."

Final Reminder

Part 3 Strategy

In Part 3:

  • opinion first
  • then reason
  • then example
  • then result

Best Structure:

  • • I think...
  • • One reason is that...
  • • For example...
  • • So overall...

Model

"I think practical skills are very important for young people today. One reason is that these skills are useful in both education and future work. For example, communication and digital skills are needed in many modern jobs. So overall, practical skills have become very valuable."

Final High-Value Topics (1/2)

Topic 1: Work / Field

Main Ideas:

Civil engineering, architecture, design, restoration, meaningful work.

"I work in civil engineering and architecture. I like this field because it combines practical work with creativity. For example, I am especially interested in design and useful projects. So overall, it is a field that suits me very well."

Topic 2: Future Goal

Main Ideas:

IELTS, speaking improvement, Italy, master’s degree, future study.

"One of my main goals is to improve my English and get a suitable IELTS band score. This is very important for me because I want to continue my studies in Italy. So this goal is closely connected to my future."

Topic 3: Free Time

Main Ideas:

Fashion content, social media, visual ideas, creative expression.

"In my free time, I mostly enjoy creating fashion-related content for social media. I like it because it feels creative and personal. For example, I sometimes think about visual style and short content ideas. So this is one of my favourite activities."

Final High-Value Topics (2/2)

Topic 4: Influence

Main Ideas:

  • Sister
  • Architecture student
  • University visit
  • Future effect

"A person who influenced me a lot is my sister. She studied architecture, and when I visited her university, I was very impressed by the environment. That experience made me more interested in architecture and engineering. So she had a strong influence on me."

Topic 5: Skill to Improve

Main Ideas:

  • English speaking
  • IELTS
  • Clear speaking
  • Future use

"The skill I would most like to improve is English speaking. I need it for IELTS and for my future academic plans. At the moment, I am practising more regularly and trying to speak more clearly. So this is one of the most important skills for me now."

Final Exam Language Bank

Part 1

  • • Yes, I do.
  • • Not really, to be honest.
  • • Most of the time, yes.
  • • It depends, but generally...
  • • One reason is that...
  • • For example...
  • • So overall...

Part 2

  • • I’d like to talk about...
  • • The person/skill I want to describe is...
  • • This happened when...
  • • What happened was...
  • • I remember feeling...
  • • What made it important was...
  • • That is why I still remember it.

Part 3

  • • I think / In my opinion...
  • • One reason is that...
  • • Another reason is that...
  • • For example...
  • • On one hand / On the other hand...
  • • So overall...
  • • That is why I think that.

Emergency Rescue Phrases

If you need thinking time:

  • • Let me think.
  • • That’s an interesting question.
  • • I’d say...
  • • In my case...

If you do not understand:

  • • Sorry, could you repeat the question, please?
  • • Sorry, I didn’t catch the question.

If you get stuck:

  • • What I mean is...
  • • In other words...
  • • For example...
  • • A simple example is...
  • • So overall...

Important Final Rule

If you make a grammar mistake: do not stop do not panic continue speaking

Survival Guide

Common Final Mistakes to Avoid:

  • answering too fast
  • giving too many ideas in one answer
  • stopping after one sentence
  • forgetting the example in Part 3
  • changing topic in Part 2
  • trying to use difficult English and becoming unclear
  • panicking after one mistake

If the question is EASY:

Answer naturally → add one reason → add one detail.

If the question is DIFFICULT:

Simplify it → answer safely → use one clear idea → keep speaking.

If Part 2 or 3 feels difficult:

P2: Choose the simplest real example from your life. Do not invent complicated stories.

P3: Use Opinion + Reason + Example + Result.

You Are Ready.

Final Confidence Checklist

Before the exam, remember:

  • I do not need perfect English.
  • I need clear English.
  • I already have useful real-life topics.
  • I can answer with one clear idea.
  • I can use safe speaking structures.
  • I can continue even if I make a mistake.

Final Rapid Review Questions

Part 1 Review

What kind of work do you do? • What do you do in your free time? • Do you use social media often? • Has your hometown changed?

Part 2 Review

Describe a skill you would like to improve. • Describe a person who influenced you. • Describe a goal you want to achieve.

Part 3 Review

Why do some people learn skills faster than others? • Should schools teach more practical skills? • Has technology changed communication? • Do young people feel pressure about the future?

Final Model Answers

Part 1 Model

Q: What do you do in your free time?

In my free time, I mostly enjoy creating fashion-related content for social media. I like it because it gives me a creative way to express my ideas. For example, I sometimes think about outfits and visual content. So it is one of my favourite activities.

Part 2 Model

Q: Describe a goal you want to achieve.

A goal I would really like to achieve is getting a suitable IELTS band score and improving my English speaking. This is important for me because I want to continue my studies in Italy. At the moment, I am practising regularly and trying to build clearer and more organised answers. I feel motivated because this goal can open important opportunities for my future. That is why it matters so much to me.

Part 3 Model

Q: Why do some people learn skills faster than others?

I think some people learn skills faster because they practise more regularly and with more focus. One reason is that repeated practice helps people improve step by step and become more confident. For example, a person who uses English every day usually improves faster than someone who studies only occasionally. So overall, regular and focused practice makes a big difference.

Trust Your Preparation

Trust your real-life ideas.

Trust your simple structure.

You DO NOT need:

perfect grammar
very advanced vocabulary
long complicated answers

You NEED:

calm speaking
clear ideas
simple structure
confidence

Last Homework: Light Review

  • listen to your best answers
  • repeat your strongest Part 2 topics
  • review your useful speaking phrases
  • do one short warm-up before the exam
  • sleep well and stay calm

DO NOT:

  • do a very long stressful practice
  • try to memorise too many new words
  • overload yourself before the exam

You Are Ready.

In the exam:

Answer the question.

Use one main idea.

Give one reason.

Give one example.

Keep going.

That is enough to help your speaking sound stronger and more confident. Good luck!
