IELTS Speaking

Lesson 9

Part 2 Storytelling
& Long Answer Development

Main Goals

Today we will focus on how to:

1
Speak for 1.5–2 minutes in Part 2
2
Continue speaking without stopping
3
Turn short ideas into a full story
4
Improve sequence, detail, & extension

Part 2 is not about speaking fast. It is about telling one clear story in an organised way.

Warm-Up

Answer these 2 easy speaking questions first:

1. Tell me about a memorable day from your life.

2. Tell me about a person who helped you.

Teacher Note

Today we are focusing on speaking longer in Part 2.

You do not need difficult English. You need a clear order.

Today's Core Rule

Do NOT say:

  • many small disconnected ideas
  • the same sentence again and again
  • only the bullet points with no development

Instead, DO:

  • choose one clear story
  • follow time order
  • add simple details
  • explain feelings and result

One story. One direction. One ending.

Do not change topic in the middle.

Part 2 Structure (The 5 Steps)

Part 2 Golden Formula

Opening Background Main Event Feeling Importance

1

Opening

Introduce the topic

2

Background

Who / where / when

3

Main Story

Say what happened

4

Feeling

Say how you felt

5

Importance

Why it was important

Bad Style vs. Better Style

Bad Style (Just a list of words)

"She is my sister and architecture and university and I like design and friends and atmosphere and project and teacher and that’s all."

Better Style (A connected story)

"I’d like to talk about my sister, who influenced me a lot. I visited her university when I was younger, and that was the first time I saw architecture students working on real projects. I remember feeling very impressed because the environment looked serious and creative at the same time. After that visit, I became more interested in architecture and engineering. That is why this experience stayed in my mind."

Teaching Point 1

How to Expand a Short Idea

Every short sentence can grow.

Short

She influenced me.

Longer

She influenced me because she studied architecture.

Better

She influenced me because she studied architecture, and when I visited her university, I saw how seriously she worked on her projects.

Extended (Band 6+)

She influenced me because she studied architecture, and when I visited her university, I saw how seriously she worked on her projects. At that time, I was still young, so that experience made a strong impression on me and changed how I thought about my future.

Teaching Points 2 & 3

Use Time Order

Part 2 becomes easier when you speak in time order.

First... At that time... After that... Then... Later... In the end... Finally...

First, I went there with my family.

After that, I saw the place and met some people.

Then, I started to feel more interested.

In the end, I realised that this experience was very important for me.

Add 4 Types of Details

  • 1

    Place detail

    Where it happened, what it looked like.

    Ex: I visited her university in another city.

  • 2

    People detail

    Who was there, what kind of person.

    Ex: I saw her classmates and teachers.

  • 3

    Feeling detail

    How you felt at the moment.

    Ex: I felt very impressed and excited.

  • 4

    Result detail

    What changed after that.

    Ex: After that, I became more interested in architecture.

Teaching Point 4

Part 2 is a Story, Not a List

The bullet points are only a guide. Do not answer them like a checklist.

Not Good (Checklist)

  • who this person is = my sister
  • how I know her = she is my sister
  • what she did = architecture
  • why she influenced me = I like architecture

Better (Story)

"The person I want to talk about is my sister. I have known her all my life, of course, because she is a close family member. What made her special to me was that she studied architecture, and I once had the chance to visit her university. That visit changed the way I thought about my own future, so she had a strong influence on me."

Useful Part 2 Phrases

Opening

  • • I’d like to talk about...
  • • A good example that comes to mind is...
  • • The person I want to describe is...
  • • The experience I want to talk about is...

Background

  • • I have known this person for...
  • • This happened when I was...
  • • It took place in...
  • • At that time, ...

Main Event

  • • What happened was...
  • • First of all, ...
  • • After that, ...
  • • Then I noticed that...
  • • Another thing I remember is...

Feeling

  • • I felt really...
  • • I was surprised because...
  • • I felt impressed by...
  • • It made me feel...

Importance

• It was important because... • I learned that... • Since then, ... • That’s why I still remember it clearly.

Controlled Practice

1. Expand One Idea

Start with: "I visited my sister’s university."

Expand step by step:

  • when?
  • where?
  • why?
  • what did you see?
  • how did you feel?
  • why was it important?

2. Speak in Order

Topic: Describe a person who influenced you.

Teacher gives prompts, student answers in full sentences:

  • who?
  • when?
  • what happened?
  • feeling?
  • result?

Practice Topic 1

Describe a person who influenced you.

Safe Answer

I’d like to talk about my sister. She influenced me because she studied architecture. When I was younger, I visited her university and saw her projects. I felt very impressed by that environment. After that, I became more interested in architecture and engineering. That is why she influenced me a lot.

Better Answer

I’d like to talk about my sister, who influenced me in a very positive way. I have known her all my life because she is a close family member, and I have always respected her. The reason she influenced me so much is that she studied architecture, which is a field I became interested in later. When I was younger, I had the chance to visit her university in another city. While I was there, I saw her projects, some of her classmates, and the general atmosphere of the university. I remember feeling really impressed because everything looked creative, serious, and meaningful at the same time. Before that visit, architecture was just an idea for me, but after that, it started to feel like a real future path. That is why this experience stayed in my mind and why my sister had such a strong influence on me.

Practice Topic 2

Describe a skill you would like to improve.

Safe Answer

The skill I would like to improve is English speaking. I want to improve it because I need IELTS for my future studies. Right now, I am practising with my teacher and trying to speak more clearly. This skill can help me in university and in my future career. So it is very important for me.

Better Answer

The skill I would most like to improve is English speaking. This is very important for me because I need IELTS for my future studies, especially because I want to continue my education abroad. At the moment, I am practising regularly with my teacher, learning useful speaking phrases, and trying to organise my answers better. One thing I am learning is how to focus on one main idea instead of saying too many disconnected things. I know that if I improve this skill, it will help me not only in the IELTS exam, but also in future study, communication, and professional situations. That is why I see speaking as one of the most important skills for me right now.

Practice Topic 3

Describe a place where you feel relaxed.

Safe Answer

A place where I feel relaxed is a quiet natural place. I go there when I want to feel calm and think clearly. I like it because it is peaceful and has fresh air. That is why I feel relaxed there.

Better Answer

A place where I feel relaxed is a quiet natural area that I sometimes visit when I want to feel calm and think clearly. It is not a very crowded place, which is one of the reasons I like it so much. I usually go there when I need a break from stress or daily routine. What makes it special for me is the peaceful atmosphere, the fresh air, and the fact that I can slow down mentally when I am there. In busy daily life, people often do not realise how much they need calm spaces. That is why this place helps me relax so much and why I always remember it positively.

Part 2 Rescue Technique (If Stuck)

  • "What I remember most is..."
  • "Another thing I can say is..."
  • "I also remember that..."
  • "What made it special was..."
  • "The reason it stayed in my mind is..."

Review + Homework

Today's Most Important Rule:

One story + clear order + simple detail = strong Part 2 answer

Final Review Message:

  • In Part 2, do not try to sound difficult.
  • Try to sound clear.
  • Tell one story.
  • Speak in order.
  • Add feelings and result.
  • Finish strongly.

Voice Homework

Please send 2 recordings:

  • 1. Describe a person who influenced you.
  • 2. Describe a skill you would like to improve.

Homework Rules:

  • Speak for 1.5 to 2 minutes
  • Use one clear story / direction
  • Do not repeat the same idea
  • Use time order
  • Add one feeling & one result
  • Finish clearly

Keep It Simple.

In Part 2, you do not need perfect English.

You need a clear story.

  • Start clearly.
  • Continue in order.
  • Add feeling.
  • Say why it mattered.

This will make your answer longer, stronger, and easier to understand!
