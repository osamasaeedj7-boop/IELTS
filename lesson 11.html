Teaching Point 1

If You Do Not Understand the Question

In IELTS, you can ask for repetition. That is normal. But you must ask politely and clearly.

Safe Phrases to Use:

  • "Sorry, could you repeat the question, please?"
  • "Sorry, I didn’t catch that."
  • "Could you say that again, please?"
  • "Sorry, could you repeat the last part?"
  • "Do you mean...?"

Do NOT say:

"What?" / "Again?" / "I don't know."

Do not stay silent!

Better Style:

"Sorry, could you repeat the question, please?"

(Examiner repeats, then you answer).

Teaching Point 2

If You Understand But Have No Idea

If you have no strong idea, use a safe general idea. A simple answer is better than silence.

Part 1 Rescue

  • • Most of the time, yes...
  • • Not really, to be honest...
  • • It depends, but generally...

Part 2 Rescue

  • • A good example that comes to mind is...
  • • One experience I remember is...
  • • I do not have a perfect example, but I can talk about...

Part 3 Rescue

  • • I think one main reason is...
  • • In general, I would say...
  • • It depends on the situation, but...

Example

Q: Do you enjoy reading poetry?

"To be honest, not really. I do read sometimes, but I usually prefer practical or informative things. For example, I like reading something that gives me a clear benefit. So poetry is not my first choice."

Teaching Points 3 & 4

Getting Stuck & Self-Correction

If you get stuck (Bridge Phrases)

Do not stop completely. Use a bridge phrase and continue.

Part 1 Another reason is that... / In my daily life... / So that’s why...
Part 2 Another thing I remember is... / What made it special was... / The reason it stayed in my mind is...
Part 3 One reason is that... / Another point is that... / So overall...

How to Correct Yourself Naturally

Small self-correction is okay. Do it quickly and continue.

  • "Sorry, I mean..."
  • "Or rather..."
  • "Let me say that again."
  • "What I mean is..."
"I went there in 2022... sorry, I mean 2021. It was during my high school period."

Mini Full Mock Test

Part 1 (Work/Free Time)

  • What kind of work do you do?
  • Why did you choose this job?
  • What do you do in your free time?
  • Do hobbies help people relax?
  • Do you use social media regularly?
  • What do you usually use it for?

Part 2 (Topic)

Describe a skill you would like to improve.

You should say:

  • - what the skill is
  • - why you want to improve it
  • - what you are doing now
  • - and explain how it can help you

Part 3 (Follow-up)

  • Why do some people learn skills faster than others?
  • Is practice more important than talent?
  • What skills are important for young people today?
  • Should schools teach more practical skills?

Teacher Checklist During Mock:

Avoid jumping ideas? Speak in order? Give opinion/reason/example? End clearly? Ask politely if stuck?

Post-Mock: Rebuilding Weak Answers

Part 1 Rebuild

Q: What do you do in your free time?

Weak

I do many things, guitar, swimming, social media, many hobbies.

Rebuilt

"In my free time, I mostly enjoy creating fashion content for social media. I like it because it feels creative and personal. For example, I sometimes plan outfits and shoot short videos. So that is one of my favourite activities."

Part 2 Rebuild

Q: Describe a skill you would like to improve.

Weak

I want improve English speaking because IELTS and Italy and future and teacher and study.

Rebuilt

"The skill I would most like to improve is English speaking. I want to improve it because I need IELTS for my future studies, especially because I want to continue my education abroad. Right now, I am practising regularly with my teacher and trying to organise my answers more clearly. If I improve this skill, it will help me in university, communication, and my future career. So it is one of the most important goals for me now."

Part 3 Rebuild

Q: Why do some people learn skills faster than others?

Weak

Because skills important and some people work more and talent and teacher and life.

Rebuilt

"I think some people learn skills faster because they practise more regularly. One reason is that repeated practice helps them improve step by step. For example, if a person speaks English every day, they usually become more fluent than someone who practises only once a week. So overall, regular practice makes a big difference."

Time Control & Breathing

Best Speed Strategy:

  • Calm
  • Clear
  • Natural
  • Connected

If too fast = weak ideas. If too slow = lose flow. Aim for steady speaking.

Breathing / Calming Strategy:

Before answering:

  1. take a tiny breath
  2. start with a safe phrase
  3. continue with one main idea

Exam Survival Drill

Train your response to these 5 "problem moments".

1. You did not hear the question

"Sorry, could you repeat the question, please?"

2. You forgot your idea

"Let me think. I’d say one important reason is..."

3. You made a grammar mistake

"Sorry, I mean..."

4. Your Part 2 is becoming too short

"Another thing I remember is..."

"What made it special was..."

5. Your Part 3 answer is too general

"For example..."

"This is especially true for..."

High-Value Model Answers

Part 1

Do you use social media regularly?

Yes, I do. I use social media quite regularly because it is useful for both communication and creative ideas. For example, I often watch fashion-related content and sometimes share my own work. So overall, it is an important part of my daily routine.

Part 3

Should schools teach more practical skills?

Yes, I think schools should teach more practical skills. One reason is that students need useful abilities for real life, not only academic information. For example, communication, time management, and problem-solving are important in both work and daily life. So overall, I believe practical skills should have a bigger place in education.

Part 2

Describe a skill you would like to improve.

The skill I would most like to improve is English speaking. This is very important for me because I need IELTS for my future studies, and I also want to feel more confident in communication. At the moment, I am practising with my teacher and trying to speak in a clearer and more organised way. One thing I am learning is how to focus on one main idea instead of saying too many disconnected points. If I improve this skill, it will help me in university, daily communication, and my future career. That is why I see it as one of the most important skills for me right now.

Review + Homework

Today's Most Important Rules:

  • If you do not understand, ask politely.
  • If you get stuck, use a bridge phrase.
  • If you make a mistake, correct quickly and continue.
  • If your idea is weak, make it simple and clear.
  • In IELTS, control is more important than perfection.

Voice Homework

Please send 3 recordings:

  • Part 1: Do you prefer studying alone or with other people?
  • Part 2: Describe a skill you would like to improve.
  • Part 3: Should schools teach more practical skills?

Homework Rules:

  • If you get stuck, do not stop
  • Use a rescue phrase
  • Continue speaking
  • Keep one main idea
  • Finish clearly

Stay Calm & Keep Control.

In the real exam, you do not need zero mistakes.

You need calm control.

  • If you do not understand, ask.
  • If you forget, continue.
  • If you make a mistake, correct it and move on.

This is how strong speaking works in real IELTS!
